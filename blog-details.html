How to Choose the Perfect Reference Photo for Your Pet Portrait

Elena Vasquez | May 10, 2026

Choosing the right photo is the single most important step in the commission process. As an artist, I rely on your photos to see the details that make your pet unique—the texture of their fur, the sparkle in their eyes, and their characteristic expression.

1. Lighting is Everything

The best photos are taken in natural light. Harsh indoor lighting or camera flashes can flatten your pet's features and distort their natural colors. Ideally, take your photos outdoors on a slightly overcast day or indoors near a large window.

"A good photo captures the likeness; a great photo captures the spirit. Lighting is the bridge between the two."

2. Eye Level is Key

Avoid taking photos from above (standing over your pet). This creates an unnatural perspective. Instead, crouch down or sit on the floor so the camera is at eye level with your pet. This creates a much more intimate and engaging portrait.

3. High Resolution Matters

Modern smartphones are excellent, but make sure you're sending the original, full-size file. Photos saved from social media are often compressed and lose the fine details I need to paint realistic fur or whiskers.
